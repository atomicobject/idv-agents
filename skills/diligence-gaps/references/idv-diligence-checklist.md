## Page 1: Data Room Checklist

> Successful fundraising is an exercise of effectively managing relationships and sharing the story of how your business is primed to grow. A critical part of the storytelling process is building a organized dataroom. Your dataroom should transparently help investors understand your vision, your key relationships, and how you've navigated your path to be a strong candidate for investment. Here are key documents to include in a typical data room:

### Business Overview
- Pitch Deck
- Team bios (% of time contributed)
- 2-3 References (developers, customers, etc)
- Sales or prospect pipeline (if applicable)
- Technical documentation (if applicable, essential for deep tech and life sciences)

### Financials
- 3-5 Financial/Growth Model
- Current Year Monthly Budget/Cash Flow Burn, short-term projections
- P&L (ideally reconciled)
- Most Recent Balance Sheet (ideally reconciled)
- Most Recent Income Statement (ideally reconciled)

### Fundraising
- Term Sheet (if applicable)
- Detailed Use of Funds
- Current Cap Table and Vesting Schedule
- Current/Outstanding SAFE/CN

### Corporate and Legal Docs
- Formation Documents (ByLaws, Articles of Inc., Certificate of inc, etc.)
- IP/Patents/Trademarks
- Employee Agreement/Separation Agreement/Non-compete
- Current Executed Contracts (or sample contract) — perhaps a working template you have for customers, service providers, etc.

## Page 2: Information we are evaluating in Diligence

### Team & Execution
- Key personnel — industry/sector experience, start-up experience, execution track record
- Understanding team dynamics — who's on it, different roles, who holds power, etc
- Plans for future hires
- Key advisors

### Business Model
- Overview of revenue streams/model
- Pricing and/or contracts
- Unit Economics
- Go-to-market strategy

### Market
- Total Market Size (top down)
- (Most important) Bottom up, serviceable market analysis

### Competitor Analysis
- Who are key competitors
- Brief overview of each competitor
- How company differentiates from each competitor
- Barriers to entry

### Technology & Product
- Product Overview
- Description of Technology (Industry Specific)
- Product Roadmap

### Financials
- Deal Overview
- Funding Status (funds raised so far)
- Use of Funds
- Burn & Runway
- Financial Projections with logical assumptions
- Cap table
- Exit opportunity
