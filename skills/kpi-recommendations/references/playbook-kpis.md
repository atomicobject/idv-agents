## §1.3 KPIs by Company Type (p.4–5)

> Different business models require different proof points. The table below suggests the primary KPIs investors will focus on by company type and stage.

| Company Type | Seed / Pre-Seed KPIs | Series A KPIs |
|---|---|---|
| B2B SaaS | MRR/ARR, MoM growth, churn, ACV, pipeline | ARR, net revenue retention, CAC payback, LTV/CAC, logo count, expansion revenue |
| Marketplace | GMV, take rate, liquidity, supply/demand growth | GMV growth, unit economics, repeat rates, geographic expansion, contribution margin |
| Consumer / PLG | DAU/MAU, activation rate, retention curves, virality (K-factor) | Retention cohorts, revenue per user, CAC/LTV, engagement depth, paywall conversion |
| Biotech / Deep Tech | Technical milestones, IP portfolio, regulatory path, team pedigree | Clinical/trial progress, partnership pipeline, grant funding, path to commercialization |
| Fintech | TPV, revenue, regulatory status, default/loss rates | TPV growth, net revenue margin, compliance track record, cohort performance, unit economics |
| Hardware / Robotics | Prototype status, bill of materials cost, LOIs/pre-orders | Units shipped, gross margin trajectory, supply chain reliability, reorder rates |

## §1.1 What a Great Investor Update Includes (p.3) — for context

A monthly investor update should include:

| Section | What It Contains | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Headline metrics | Revenue/ARR, growth rate, burn, runway, key conversion rates | Gives investors a snapshot of trajectory |
| Product progress | Shipped features, roadmap updates, milestones hit | Demonstrates execution velocity |
| Customer wins | Logos closed, expansion deals, notable usage examples | Shows market pull and validates ICP |
| Team updates | New hires, departures, key role searches | Signals execution capacity |
| Risks & challenges | Honest naming of what's not working and what's being done about it | Builds trust; investors notice when things are only ever rosy |
| Forward-looking milestones | What you're aiming to hit by next update | Lets investors track promise vs. delivery |
| Specific asks | Named intros, hiring help, customer intros, advice on a specific problem | Activates the network the distribution list represents |
| Financial snapshot | Cash position, monthly burn, months of runway | Investor-grade financial visibility |

KPI recommendations should slot into the **Headline metrics** section of this structure. The skill should remind founders that the same headline metrics should appear in **every** monthly update so trends are trackable.

## §1.1 Mistakes to Avoid (p.4) — investor-update anti-patterns

> **Mistakes to Avoid:**
> - Vanity metrics without context (e.g., "10,000 signups" with no mention of activation or retention).
> - Sending updates only when things are going well. Investors notice the gaps.
> - Overly polished, marketing-style updates that feel inauthentic.
> - Not including specific asks, which wastes the networking power of your distribution list.
> - Waiting until 3 months before the raise to start building investor relationships.
> - Sharing different metrics each update, making it impossible to track trends.
> - Not tracking who opens and engages with your updates.

The two mistakes the skill flags directly when reviewing a founder's current KPI set:

1. **"Vanity metrics without context (e.g., '10,000 signups' with no mention of activation or retention)."**
2. **"Sharing different metrics each update, making it impossible to track trends."**

### Calibration: what counts as a vanity metric

A reach/social/awareness number is **not automatically** a vanity metric. The playbook anti-pattern triggers only when the number appears **without funnel-conversion or revenue-attribution context**.

- **NOT vanity**: "1.4M social impressions → 20K followers → email list grew 8% MoM → email channel drove 44% of revenue at $X CAC." The reach number is wrapped in a conversion funnel and tied to revenue. This is the pattern the skill should reinforce, not flag.
- **Vanity (flag)**: "10,000 signups" with no activation rate. "1M impressions" with no follower-to-customer conversion. "500 followers" with no engagement, list growth, or revenue link.

The skill should name vanity metrics plainly when they appear, but should NOT false-positive on legitimate funnel-anchored reach numbers.

## §7.4 Where AI Helps and Where It Hurts (p.20) — cross-cutting

| AI Should Help With | AI Should NOT Do |
|---|---|
| Drafting initial outreach templates that the founder edits | Sending any message autonomously |
| Summarizing investor backgrounds for personalization | Generating mass-personalized outreach at scale (investors can tell) |
| Suggesting follow-up timing based on engagement signals | Deciding which investors to deprioritize or drop |
| Identifying talking points based on investor interests | Fabricating connections or shared interests that do not exist |
| Formatting and proofreading | Replacing the founder's authentic voice with AI-generated language |

For kpi-recommendations specifically:

- **AI Should NOT** invent KPIs that aren't in §1.3 or fabricate benchmark numbers ("good NPS is 40+") without honest framing as general industry guidance.
- **AI Should NOT** decide for the founder which KPIs they "really" should track — present the §1.3 list, let the founder calibrate with their lead investor.
- The caveat footer the skill emits reflects this: KPI recommendations are a starting point, not a verdict.
